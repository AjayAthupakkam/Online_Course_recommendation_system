Here is the complete, production-ready architecture flow for Option 2 (Retrieval + Machine Learning Ranking).This two-stage design is exactly how major tech companies handle massive datasets—using a fast mathematical model to filter down the catalog, and a powerful machine learning model to fine-tune the final recommendations.The Two-Stage Hybrid Architecture FlowStep 1: Data Ingestion & TransformationBefore any recommendation happens, your data passes through your ColumnTransformer pipeline:Courses Table: course_name is converted via TF-IDF. Numerical features (course_price, course_duration_hours) are scaled via MinMaxScaler. Categorical features are encoded.User Interactions Table: Logs of user_id, course_id, rating, and time_spent_hours.Step 2: Stage 1 — Candidate Retrieval (The Content Filter)The goal here is speed. We want to narrow down your entire course catalog from thousands of courses to the top 50 most relevant candidates for a specific user based on their history.The Logic: Look at the courses the user has already taken or highly rated. Find other courses that have the closest vector distance.The Math: Cosine Similarity calculates the cosine of the angle between the multi-dimensional feature vectors of your target course and all other courses.$$Text\ Similarity = \cos(\theta) = \frac{\mathbf{A} \cdot \mathbf{B}}{\|\mathbf{A}\| \|\mathbf{B}\|}$$Output: A short list of 50 Candidate Courses that match the user's semantic interest. (This completely bypasses the cold-start problem for new items because we only rely on item metadata features here).Step 3: Stage 2 — Scoring & Ranking (The Machine Learning Layer)The goal here is precision. We take the 50 candidates from Step 1 and figure out exactly how much this specific user will enjoy them based on behavioral data.The Logic: You train a supervised tree-based model (LightGBM or XGBoost Regressor) where the target variable ($Y$) is the expected rating (or a computed engagement score combining rating and time_spent_hours).The Features fed to the model ($X$): * User Features: previous_courses_takenItem Features: feedback_score, enrollment_numbers, certification_offeredInteraction Metrics: Collaborative historical averages.The Process: The model predicts a continuous value (e.g., predicted rating from $1.0$ to $5.0$) for each of the 50 candidates.Step 4: Re-Ranking & Business Logic (The Final Output)Before showing the results to the user, you apply a clean Python script to sort and clean the predictions.Sorting: Sort the 50 candidates in descending order based on the LightGBM predicted score.Filtering: Remove any course_id that the user has already completed (checking against their interaction history).Output: Present the Top 5 recommended courses on the user's dashboard.Summary of the Engineering Execution Flow[User logs into Profile]
